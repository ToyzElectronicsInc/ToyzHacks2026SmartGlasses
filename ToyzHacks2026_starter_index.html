🛰️ Mission Briefing — Space Station, 2031

The smart glasses that run your AR heads-up display are dead. No power. Without them, you can't navigate the station or complete repairs. Your task: rebuild the power management circuit from salvaged components so the glasses can boot. You have a LiPo battery, a PMIC, and a decoupling capacitor. Make them work together.

Chapter 1 — The Spec

Before you build anything, read the circuit spec. The PMIC needs stable 3.3V output. The LiPo delivers 3.7V nominal. A decoupling capacitor smooths the voltage rail.
